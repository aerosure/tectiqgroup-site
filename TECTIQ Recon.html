Detailed NATCAT Exposure Analysis

The project site at 4200 Westheimer Rd, Houston, TX sits approximately 14 miles inland from the Galveston Bay coastline. While this distance provides meaningful storm surge mitigation (estimated maximum surge penetration at this location is <2 ft in a Category 5 scenario), the site remains fully exposed to hurricane-force wind fields that extend well beyond the coastal fringe in major landfalling systems.

Historical analysis of the Houston metro area shows 23 tropical cyclones passing within 100nm of this location since 1980, with Hurricane Harvey (2017) and Hurricane Ike (2008) representing the most significant recent events. The 30-month policy period spans two complete Atlantic hurricane seasons (June–November 2026 and 2027), creating compounding exposure windows.

Severe convective storm (SCS) exposure is independently elevated. Harris County averaged 4.2 significant hail events per year over the past decade, with the April–June window representing peak frequency. Construction-phase SCS exposure is particularly concerning for this project given exposed structural steel and incomplete envelope during peak hail months.

Construction Schedule Risk Analysis

The stated 30-month construction duration exceeds the benchmark range of 22–26 months for a 22-story Type II non-combustible mixed-use project of this scope ($142M TIV / 415,000 SF). Extended duration increases cumulative NATCAT exposure and introduces schedule risk premium that should be reflected in pricing.

Critical path analysis suggests structural topping out would occur approximately July 2027 — coinciding with the peak of the second hurricane season. At topping out, the structure represents maximum frame value at maximum wind vulnerability (steel erected, envelope incomplete). This confluence of construction phase and NATCAT seasonality warrants specific underwriting attention and deductible structuring.
